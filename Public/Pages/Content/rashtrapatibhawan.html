About The Rashtrapati Bhawan

Visiting the Rashtrapati Bhavan is the dream of every Indian citizen who wishes to take a tour of the massive Presidential Estate, the elegant Mughal gardens, and the newly inaugurated museum that deftly depicts the history of this building. Built during the period 1912 to 1929, this 340-room building once served as the British Viceroy's House and was first occupied by Lord Irwin during the British Raj, and later by C Rajagopalachari after India gained independence.
